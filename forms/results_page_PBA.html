LifVest recommends a personal brokerage account

We believe you are suited for a low-cost, do-it-yourself Fidelity brokerage account. In this account, you manage your money personally and can buy stocks, ETFs, mutual funds, bonds, and options. In general, LifVest recommends low-cost index funds for the majority of your money alongside a minority allocation of single stocks.

What does Fidelity charge?

Brokerage accounts make money by handling your transactions - your buys and sells. Specifically, Fidelity charges $8 per stock or ETF trade, and $50 for mutual funds. There are a number of investment products where the transaction commission is waived (certain iShares or Fidelity branded funds). Also, it is important to note that many investments you buy charge a management fee -- that is, a percentage cost to own the investment. Pay attention to the management fee for every ETF or mutual fund you buy! Broadly speaking, a personal brokerage account is the cheapest way to invest, but it sacrifices personal service, expert advice, and automation.

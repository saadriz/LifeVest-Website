LifVest recommends a personal brokerage account

We believe you are suited for a low-cost, do-it-yourself Tradestation brokerage account. In this account, you manage your money personally and can buy stocks, ETFs, mutual funds, bonds, and options. In general, LifVest recommends low-cost index funds for the majority of your money alongside a minority allocation of single stocks.

What does Tradestation charge?

Brokerage accounts make money by handling your transactions - your buys and sells. Specifically, Tradestation charges either $5 per trade or $0.01 per share -- your choice.

Also, it is important to note that many investments you buy charge a management fee -- that is, a percentage cost to own the investment. Pay attention to the management fee for every ETF or mutual fund you buy! Broadly speaking, a personal brokerage account is the cheapest way to invest, but it sacrifices personal service, expert advice, and automation.

LifVest recommends a personal financial advisor

We believe you are suited for a Morgan Stanley financial advisor to manage your investments. With a financial advisor, your investments are a constant conversation between you and your wealth manager. Since you do not have the time or desire to manage your money yourself, your private wealth manager will follow markets and invest for you. One of the most valuable aspects of using a personal financial advisor is they answer your questions about investing, while personal brokerage accounts and robo-advisors do not.

What does Morgan Stanley charge?

The costs for personal advisors is variable. Broadly speaking, personal financial advisors are more costly than other options for investing, but they provide personal service and human expertise.

LifVest recommends a Registered Investment Advisor

We believe you are best suited for a registered investment advisor to manage your investments. With an RIA, your investments are overseen by an investment professional with a public track record. Since you do not have the desire or time to invest yourself, your RIA will follow markets and rebalance for you.

What do RIAs charge?

RIAs make money by charging a management fee, a percentage of your money. The cost for independent RIAs are variable, but a typical fee is 1-2% every year. RIAs can offer unique and complex styles for investing. Lifvest recommends meeting RIA firms in person before investing.

LifVest recommends a robo-advisor account

We believe you are suited for a Wealthfront robo-advisor account. In this account, your money is invested by a third-party, Wealthfront, and is diversified into a portfolio of low-cost index funds. Wealthfront chooses a mix of assets for you out of a menu of 11 index funds (ETFs). In addition, Wealthfront rebalances the account periodically to maintain your investment mix. Since robo-advisors use a formula to invest people's money, they charge lower fees than a traditional financial advisor.

What does Wealthfront charge?

Robo-advisors make money by charging a management fee, a percentage of your money. Specifically, Wealthfront charges 0.25% a year for balances higher than $10,000. For example, if you invested $100,000 at Wealthfront, you would pay them $225 a year ($90,000 x 0.0025 ; first $10,000 is managed for free). Also, it is important to note that the ETFs Wealthfront buy for your account have management fees (generally 0.05% to 0.25% a year). These fees are reflected directly in the value of the ETFs (for a 0.05% fee, instead of getting a bill of a nickel for every $100 you had invested in that fund, the fund's value would go from $100 to $99.95). Broadly speaking, robo-advisor fees are some of the lowest found in finance, but they sacrifice personal service and flexibility.

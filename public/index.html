Online Brokerage

The original pioneers that allowed consumers to invest easily, personal online brokerages (also referred to as discount brokers) are do-it-yourself accounts that charge commissions for trades. Generally the lowest-cost method to invest, online brokerage accounts give users maximum flexibility.

Robo-advisor

Robo-advisors offer automated, algorithm-based portfolio management using index funds. By trusting a system, investors are protecting their money from their own behavioral biases. Robo-advisors provide maximum diversification and lower fees than traditional financial advisory and can be customized based on risk level.

Institutional Financial Advisor

Institutional financial advisors cater to users who are looking for expert guidance and wish to hire a professional to manage their money. The advantage of using a wirehouse financial advisor is consistency in their resources, training, and research. The five most popular wealth management firms are Bank of America Merrill Lynch, Morgan Stanley, JPMorgan, Wells Fargo and UBS.

Registered Investment Advisor

RIAs are professional advisory firms that offer personalized financial management for clients. Many independent RIAs are owned and operated by the advisors who run them. Independent RIAs typically charge a fee based on a percentage of total assets managed. This fee structure is simple and easy to understand, helping to avoid surprises.
